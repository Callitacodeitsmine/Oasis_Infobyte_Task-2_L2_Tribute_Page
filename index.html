Nikola Tesla

1856 ~ 1943

“The mind is sharper and keener in seclusion and uninterrupted solitude. Originality thrives in seclusion free of outside influences beating upon us to cripple the creative mind. Be alone—that is the secret of invention: be alone, that is when ideas are born.”

- Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist who is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system. Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, Croatia. He was a brilliant inventor and had over 300 patents to his name . Tesla's inventions and discoveries have had a profound impact on the world. He is credited with the invention of the Tesla coil, which is still used today in radio and television sets. He also developed the AC motor and transformer, which are used in power generation and transmission systems around the world . Tesla was a visionary who believed in the power of science and technology to change the world. He was a man ahead of his time and his ideas were often dismissed by his contemporaries. However, his contributions to science and technology have been recognized and celebrated around the world . In conclusion, Nikola Tesla was a brilliant inventor who made significant contributions to science and technology. His inventions and discoveries have had a profound impact on the world and continue to shape our lives today. He will always be remembered as one of the greatest inventors of all time.
